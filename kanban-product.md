Kanban in Product-Driven Development

Also works here: Product teams often use Kanban for feature development or maintenance.

How it looks different from Scrum:

Instead of 2-week sprints, features move continuously through design → development → QA → release.

Prioritization is dynamic, new tasks can be pulled as capacity allows.

Useful for maintenance-heavy products or teams that release frequently.

Benefits:

More flexible than Scrum if priorities change often.

Easier to track flow and bottlenecks.

Can integrate with Agile principles (iterative development, regular retrospectives).
